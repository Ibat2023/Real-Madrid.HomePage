The History of Real Madrid

The early days of the club

Real Madrid Club de Fútbol is a professional football club based in Madrid, Spain. The club was formed in 1902 as Madrid Football Club , and played its first competitive match on 13 May 1902, when it lost 3–1 in the semi-final of the Campeonato de Copa de S.M. Alfonso XIII against FC Barcelona. Real Madrid played against other local clubs in various regional tournaments, that served as qualifiers for the Copa del Rey, from 1902 to 1940. In 1929, the club became one of the founding members of La Liga, Spain's first national league. As of 2022, Real is one of only three clubs never to have been relegated from the top level of Spanish football, the others being Athletic Bilbao and Barcelona.

The Bernabeu Era 1943-1978

Before becoming president in 1943, Santiago Bernabeu had already carried out the functions of player, first-team captain, club maintenance, first-team manager and director in an association with the club that would last nearly 70 years. Bernabéu embarked upon a strategy of signing world-class players from Spain and abroad, the most prominent being Argentine Alfredo di Stéfano. During Bernabéu's presidency, many of Real Madrid's most legendary names played for the club, including Di Stéfano, Ferenc Puskás, Francisco Gento, Héctor Rial, Raymond Kopa, José Santamaría, Miguel Muñoz, Amancio and Santillana. It was under Bernabéu's guidance that Real Madrid established itself as a major force both in Spanish and European football. Before passing away in 1978, Bernabéu had been the club's president for almost 35 years, during which his club won 1 Intercontinental Cup, 6 European Cups, 16 league titles, 6 Spanish Cups, 2 Latin Cups, and 1 Copa Eva Duarte.

Florentino Perez era 2000-2006/2009-present

Florentino Pérez returned world prestige to Real Madrid. In his two stints at the helm of the club he has won 54 titles between the two sections, including six European Cups and two basketball cups. Engineer and businessman, he arrived in July 2000 to the presidency after defeating the then white leader, Lorenzo Sanz. With his management came star signings such as Figo, Zidane, Beckham or Ronaldo. In 2009 he returns and begins a second stage marked in sports by signings such as Cristiano Ronaldo, Benzema, Bale, Luka Modric or Kroos. Among the titles won are five European Cups (2014, 2016, 2017, 2018 and 2022) and four Club World Cups. These successes are joined by four Leagues, four European Super Cups, two King's Cups and four Spanish Super Cups in football. Florentino Pérez has been managing the club for more than a decade and has continued the modernization process with the construction of the residences of the first team and the quarry within the Real Madrid City.
